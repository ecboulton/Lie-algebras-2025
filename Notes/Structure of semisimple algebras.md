## Cartan subalgebras
### Definition
Let $\mathfrak{h}$ be a Lie subalgebra of a Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$. This subalgebra is called **Cartan** if $\mathfrak{h}$ is [[Simple, semisimple, solvable, nilpotent|nilpotent]] and equal to its [[Centraliser and normaliser|normaliser]], i.e. $\mathfrak{h}=N_{\mathfrak{g}} \mathfrak{h}=\{ x \in \mathfrak{g} \;  | \; [x,\mathfrak{h}] \subseteq \mathfrak{h} \}$.

### Properties 
#### Existence
Every Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ has at least one Cartan subalgebra. 
#### Conjugacy
For all Cartan subalgebras $\mathfrak{h}_{1}, \mathfrak{h}_{2}$, there exists $g \in Aut(\mathfrak{g})$ such that $g(\mathfrak{h}_{1})=\mathfrak{h}_{2}$.
#### Cartan subalgebras of semisimple Lie algebras
If $\mathfrak{g}$ is [[Simple, semisimple, solvable, nilpotent]] and $\mathfrak{h < g}$ is a Cartan subalgebra, then:
1. $\mathfrak{h}$ is abelian.
2. $\mathfrak{h}$ is maximally abelian. [[Centraliser and normaliser|That is]], $C_{\mathfrak{g}} (\mathfrak{h}) = \mathfrak{h}$, i.e. elements of $\mathfrak{h}$ commute only with other elements of $\mathfrak{h}$.
3. All elements of $\mathfrak{h}$ are semisimple.
4. The [[Killing form]] $\kappa_{\mathfrak{g}} |_{\mathfrak{h \times h}} : \mathfrak{h \times h} \to F$ is non-degenerate.


## Root decomposition
### Roots
#### Definition
Let $\mathfrak{h < g}$ be a Cartan subalgebra of a semisimple Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$. 

Let $\mathfrak{h}$ act on $\mathfrak{g}$ by the [[Adjoint maps|adjoint action]] $\text{ad}: \mathfrak{g \times g \to g}$, $\text{ad}_{x}: y \mapsto [x,y]$, where to get the action of $\mathfrak{h}$ on $\mathfrak{g}$ we set $x \in \mathfrak{h}$, or equivalently, restrict the map with $\text{ad}|_{\mathfrak{h}}$.
